What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you
need to do it?

	The system is ready to work for version-control and 
	save the files in system as per the git rules. We have
	to initialize the repository to begin the process.

How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it
offers?
	
	Staging area is the intermediate of the two. It helps
	us to track the list of files needed to be committed
	before doing so.

How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one
commit per logical change?

	By comparing the files in staging area and most recent
	commit.

	They all can work with different branches.
